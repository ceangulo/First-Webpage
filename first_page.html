What is personal finance?

Personal finances is how we manage our money and financial resources to achieve our goals. It involves making informed decisions on our earnings, spending, savings and the investing of our money. Remeber that personal finance is about making choices tha aligh with our values and goals. It is an ongoing process that require attention and adjustments as our life circunstances changes.


Three Key aspects of personal finance

Budgeting: Create a plan for how you will use your money, outlining income and expenses. By creating a budget it will help you control your spendings to ensure you have enough for your needs.

Savings: Settimg aside a portion of your income for future needs or emergencies. A good rule of thumb is 10% of your income. Savings act as a financial safety net and can help us achive specific goals.

Investing: Growing your wealth by putting your money into vehicles that have the potential for long-term returns, these can be stocks,bonds, or retirement accounts.


Four vehicles to improve our finances here in Canada

RRSPs: The Registered Retirement Saving Plan, is a tax-advantage investment account vehicle available to all Canadian residents.

Non-Registered: This type of accoounts are often referred to simply as a taxable account, is an investment account that is not registered with the government for special tax treatment.

TFSA account: A Tax-Free Savings Account is a Canadian government-sponsored savings investment account with an annual limit and provides tax benefits for elegible individuals.

Universal Life / Permanent Tax Shelter: This is a type of permanent insurance that conbines a death benefit with an investment component. It provides a death benefit to beneficiaries, similar to any tradidional life insurance, but it also has a cash value component that earns interest and allows flexibility in premium payments.
